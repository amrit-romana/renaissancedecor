01
Brand Positioning
& Strategy

Renaissance Decor is not a plastering company. It is a luxury craft practice — one that has spent more than twenty years building the kind of reputation that cannot be bought, only earned. This document exists to protect and extend that reputation across every surface the brand touches.

"They are experienced. They operate at the top end. They are one of the best."

MARKET POSITION

Renaissance Decor occupies the uppermost tier of the Melbourne plastering market. The business is not positioned against other plasterers — it is positioned against the standard of the homes and spaces it works within. When a builder specifies Renaissance Decor, they are making a statement about the calibre of their project.

PRIMARY — LUXURY SPECIALIST
High-end builders, architects, and interior designers. Ultra-high-net-worth private residential clients across Toorak, South Yarra, Armadale, and the Mornington Peninsula. Projects are large, multi-room, and repeat.

KEY ACCOUNTS
Luxury builders (Lowe Design & Build tier). Leading interior designers — Sally Caroline, Kate Walker. Premium commercial developers — Aesop, Chanel, Mecca, David Jones-tier retail.

SECONDARY — BOUTIQUE ARTISAN
Metal finishes, clay commissions, and technically demanding creative work. Creatively valuable — but never the headline positioning. Secondary in all external communications.

NOT PURSUED
Mid-market residential. Volume rendering. Small-scale bathroom renovations. The team structure, overhead, and reputation are built exclusively for high-end, large-scale work.

THE IDEAL CLIENT

Renaissance Decor's clients share one defining trait: they know the difference quality makes, and they are prepared to pay for it. They are not comparing quotes. They are selecting a long-term partner.

The Architect / Builder
Lowe Design & Build tier. Specifies Renaissance across multiple projects each year. Relationship-driven — trust is the primary currency. High-value repeat account.
The Interior Designer
Sally Caroline, Kate Walker tier. Deeply attuned to material and finish. Brings Renaissance into client projects as a trusted, discreet collaborator.
The Private Client
Toorak, South Yarra, Peninsula. Multiple properties, high aesthetic expectations, a referral network of equally discerning friends. Fiercely loyal once trust is established.
The Commercial Developer
Aesop, AJE, premium hospitality groups. Multi-site preferred. Projects briefed through design agencies or direct design teams. Completion quality is the brief.


02
Brand Character
& Personality

Renaissance Decor should feel like architectural European craftsmanship translated for modern Australian luxury. The brand never announces itself — it is discovered through the quality of a finished surface, the warmth of a follow-up call, the precision of a site visit.

FIVE BRAND WORDS


Refined  ·  Confident  ·  Warm  ·  Authoritative  ·  Considered

WHAT RENAISSANCE DECOR IS

ESTABLISHED
Twenty years of uninterrupted practice. Not a start-up. Not a trend. A serious business with deep roots in Melbourne's luxury construction market.

LARGE AND CAPABLE
A team structure built for scale. Renaissance Decor can resource a Toorak estate and a Chadstone flagship simultaneously. Size and capability are genuine differentiators.

TECHNICALLY UNMATCHED
Venetian plaster, micro cement, clay, and metal — applied correctly, at the right specification, in the right context. The team executes finishes that most cannot.

A man-in-a-van operation. A price-competitive generalist. A business that renders front fences and takes bathroom jobs. A brand that needs to shout to be heard.

These distinctions must be legible in every brand communication — from the website to a site proposal to an Instagram caption to a text message reply.

03
Logo System

The Renaissance Decor logo system comprises four distinct marks — each built for a specific context. The system is anchored by the existing ornate crest, a symbol of heritage and craft, paired with typography that carries it into modern luxury applications without diminishing its authority.

01 — SPLIT CREST LOGO

The primary brand mark. The ornate crest is bisected horizontally by the brand name — a heraldic composition referencing classical European craft traditions. The crest is never overlapped: it is always separated from the wordmark by a double hairline rule. The top half floats above, the bottom half anchors below, the name occupies the centre.

USED FOR
	•	Business cards (front)
	•	Formal print proposals and quotes
	•	Letterheads and official documents
	•	Embossed stationery and packaging
	•	Hero placements on large-format print
	•	Presentation folders and covers

AVAILABLE COLOURWAYS
Dark background: white crest, Parchment wordmark.
Parchment background: Espresso crest, Charcoal wordmark.
Warm Plaster background: Espresso crest, near-black wordmark.


Split Crest Logo — Primary Mark

02 — HORIZONTAL WORDMARK

Used in all digital navigation contexts — website header, email signature, digital proposals, and app icons at medium scale. The split crest appears at reduced scale as an icon to the left, separated from the text block by a fine vertical hairline.

Typography: "RENAISSANCE" in Playfair Display 400 weight, wide tracking. "D E C O R" in Jost 200 weight, ultra-wide letter spacing on a second baseline. On dark backgrounds, DECOR appears in Parchment. On light backgrounds, DECOR appears in Espresso.

Minimum size: 200px wide on digital. 60mm wide in print. Below these thresholds, the crest icon becomes indistinct and should not be used.

03 — SOCIAL PROFILE MARKS

Four distinct marks are provided for social platform profile images. Each is designed to read clearly at 110px diameter — the size at which Instagram renders profile images on a mobile feed.

Option A — Crest Circle
The full ornate crest centred within a double engraved ring. Two cardinal dots at 12 and 6 o'clock. Primary recommendation for Instagram, Facebook, and Pinterest.
Option B — Stamp Seal
Circular stamp with large italic "R" centred. "RENAISSANCE DECOR" curved along the bottom arc. "EST. 2004" beneath the R. Recommended for LinkedIn and Google Business.
Option C — RD Square
Bold italic RD monogram with architectural corner brackets. Ghost crest watermark. For platforms requiring a square crop — Twitter/X, alternative Instagram.
Option D — Light Circle
Warm plaster gradient with espresso crest. For light-context placements — Apple Business, light-mode platform appearances, Instagram Stories cover.

04 — FAVICON

The split crest on an Espresso (#241810) square at 64×64px. Below 32px, the crest loses legibility — the italic "R" only is used at 16px. Always export as .ico for browser use and .png for app icons.

THE OFFLINE CREST — EXISTING MARK

The original full ornate crest is retained exclusively for formal offline use. It is never used digitally. Appropriate contexts: debossed business cards, wax seals, embossed letterheads, formal proposals, premium packaging, and certificates of work. At print scale it communicates heritage and craft that no digital mark can match.

COLOUR USAGE RULES

NEVER USE PURE BLACK OR COLD WHITE
Pure #000000 and #FFFFFF read as corporate and cold against the warmth of this brand. Always use Charcoal (#3A2A1F) for text and Parchment (#EAE8E4) for backgrounds.

MAXIMUM THREE COLOURS PER COMPOSITION
The palette works through restraint. Dominant: Parchment. Supporting: Linen or Stone. One accent: Bark or Espresso. Exceeding this creates visual noise.

SLATE IS USED SPARINGLY
This architectural blue-grey is reserved exclusively for interactive digital states — links, hover effects, focus indicators. Never in print or static design.

BACKGROUNDS ARE ALWAYS WARM
No cool greys. No cold neutrals. Every surface references the warmth of plaster and stone. If a background colour feels cold, it is wrong.

PRINT COLOUR MATCHING
Bark (#96836D) prints closest to Pantone 7530 C. Espresso (#5B4834) closest to Pantone 7526 C. Always request a physical proof on uncoated stock before print runs.

Typography
System

Two typefaces carry the entire Renaissance Decor typographic system. One classical serif. One clean sans-serif. Each has a strictly defined role. They are never mixed on the same line, and they are never substituted without explicit approval. Discipline in typography is how the brand communicates sophistication without effort.

DISPLAY — PLAYFAIR DISPLAY / GARAMOND

A classical European serif with warmth, authority, and age. Used for all primary headings, the logotype wordmark, pull quotes, and editorial callouts. Always tracks generously — never tight-set. Communicates heritage, craft, and a European tradition without being heavy or ornate.

Digital: Playfair Display (Google Fonts, free). Print / Office: Garamond. If neither is available, Times New Roman is the fallback — but this should be the last resort.

TYPOGRAPHY RULES

NEVER SET BODY COPY IN SERIF
Playfair Display and Garamond at body size (below 16pt) lose legibility. All running text uses Jost or Arial.

TRACKING IS ALWAYS GENEROUS IN DISPLAY
Display headings track at +80 to +200 units. Never tight-set a headline. Space communicates luxury.

ITALICS SIGNAL IMPORTANCE, NOT DECORATION
Garamond italic is reserved for pull quotes, testimonials, and specific callouts. Never used for body paragraphs.

LINE HEIGHT IS ALWAYS GENEROUS
Body copy: 1.6–1.8 line height minimum. Display headings: 1.1–1.2. Never condense leading below 1.0.

ALLCAPS ONLY WITH TRACKING
When text is set in allcaps, it must be tracked at minimum +60 units. Untracked allcaps reads as aggression, not luxury.

HIERARCHY IS STRICT
Never place two display headings at the same size adjacently. The eye must understand what to read first.

06
Voice & Tone

Renaissance Decor does not sell. It presents. The brand speaks with the calm authority of a practice that has been doing this for twenty years and has nothing to prove. Every word is chosen. Every sentence earns its place on the page.

CORE PRINCIPLES

AUTHORITY, NOT ARROGANCE
Write as an expert who respects the intelligence of their audience. State facts confidently. Never condescend. Never boast. The work speaks — the words clarify.

WARMTH WITHOUT INFORMALITY
Renaissance Decor is run by real people who genuinely care. That warmth exists in the tone and the relationship — not in exclamation marks, emojis, or casual language.

PRECISION OVER VOLUME
One well-chosen sentence outperforms three adequate ones. Always reduce. Never pad. If a sentence does not earn its place, remove it.

NO SALES LANGUAGE
Never use urgency, discount framing, call-to-action pressure, or superlatives without specific substance. Never say "stunning" — describe what makes it so.

SPECIFICITY SIGNALS QUALITY
"A Toorak residence" is stronger than "a luxury home". "Venetian plaster applied over three coats, hand-burnished" is stronger than "premium finishes". The specific is always more credible than the general.


VOICE IN DIFFERENT CONTEXTS


WEBSITE COPY
Confident, editorial, unhurried. Short sentences. Active voice. The brand describes what it does and who it does it for without qualification or apology. No questions. No "we believe". Statements.

PROPOSALS & QUOTES
Precise and professional. Warm but structured. Signed by name. Reads as a document from a practice that takes its work seriously — not a form generated by software.

SOCIAL MEDIA
More direct and immediate than the website, but the same voice. Single-idea captions. Material, location, year. No hashtag stacks. No emoji in project captions. Texture in the image does the talking.

DIRECT COMMUNICATION — EMAIL, SMS
Warm and human. Adam's natural tone is already right — clear, considerate, direct. Never formal to the point of coldness. Never casual to the point of carelessness.

07
Imagery
Direction

Photography is the primary storytelling medium for Renaissance Decor. The surface is the product — and every image should feel as though it belongs in Vogue Living, Habitus, or a Pt Leo Estate publication. If the image could appear in a standard real estate listing, it is not right for this brand.

PROJECT PHOTOGRAPHY

Wide and architectural — showing the full scale and spatial context of the work. Natural light always preferred; raking late-afternoon light that reveals surface texture is ideal. Compositions are calm, unhurried, and deliberately restrained. The wall is the subject. The room breathes. Nothing competes.

Light
Soft and directional. Raking light that reveals grain and texture. Never flash photography. Never overexposed. Golden hour or overcast natural light preferred.
Colour grade
Warm, never cool. Shadows retain warmth. Highlights are creamy and controlled, not blown out. Avoid any preset filter that flattens or cools tone.
Composition
Horizon lines respected. Verticals perfectly straight. Generous negative space. The surface earns the frame — never cropped or overcrowded.
Styling
Minimal. High-quality furniture and objects if present, placed sparingly. The room must never compete with the surface finish it was built around.
Post-production
Colour grading only — no removal of architectural features, no frequency separation on wall textures, no AI-generated elements of any kind.

TEXTURE & DETAIL PHOTOGRAPHY

Close-up material imagery communicating depth, grain, and the hand of the maker. Shot with a low-angle raking light to maximise texture relief. Think: how Aesop photographs their product — applied to a wall. The trace of a clay trowel. The variation in a Venetian burnish. The reflectivity of a micro cement floor catching afternoon light.

PROCESS & PEOPLE

Used selectively on social media only — never on the website or in formal print. Authentic and unposed. Adam and the team at work, not performing for camera. These images communicate craft and humanity. They are valuable precisely because they are not trying to be anything other than honest.

WHAT WE NEVER USE

	•	Stock photography of any kind — rooms, hands, materials, lifestyle
	•	Oversaturated, heavily filtered, or HDR-processed images
	•	Busy compositions where the surface competes with furniture or objects
	•	Text-on-image treatments, graphic overlays, or branded frames over photographs
	•	Before/after comparisons with logos watermarked over the image
	•	Smartphone photography for website, proposals, or formal marketing applications
	•	Images where the Renaissance Decor team member is the subject rather than the work behind them
	•	Any AI-generated imagery used as project photography

REFERENCE AESTHETICS

When briefing photographers, reviewing content, or assessing whether an image is right for the brand, reference these aesthetics: Vogue Living Australia. Habitus Magazine. Pt Leo Estate website. Aesop product photography. Minotti furniture catalogue. Apparatus Studio lookbook. The consistent thread across all of them: restraint, material focus, warm natural light, and editorial calm.


08
Logo Usage
& Misuse

The logo is the most visible expression of the Renaissance Decor brand. Consistent, correct usage builds recognition and trust over time. Any deviation — however small — erodes the brand's perceived quality. These rules exist to protect twenty years of reputation.

CLEAR SPACE

Always maintain clear space around the logo equal to the cap height of the "R" in RENAISSANCE. No other graphic element, text block, image, or decorative element may enter this zone under any circumstances. On digital applications the minimum increases to 1.5× the cap height.

MINIMUM SIZES

Split Crest Logo
40mm minimum in print. 150px minimum on screen. Below these thresholds the tagline and crest detail become illegible.
Horizontal Wordmark
60mm minimum in print. 200px minimum on screen. Below these thresholds the crest icon and wordmark begin to merge.
Social Profile Mark
Upload at 1000×1000px minimum. Instagram renders at 110px diameter — the design is specifically calibrated for this scale.
Favicon — Crest
64×64px minimum. Use at 32px where technically required.
Favicon — R only
16×16px. The italic "R" only — the crest cannot be read at this scale.

APPROVED COLOURWAYS

On dark backgrounds
White crest + Parchment (#EAE8E4) RENAISSANCE + Parchment DECOR.
On Parchment / white
Espresso (#5B4834) crest + Charcoal (#3A2A1F) RENAISSANCE + Espresso DECOR.
On warm plaster tones
Espresso crest + near-black RENAISSANCE + Espresso DECOR.
No other combinations
Any colourway not listed above has not been approved. Do not improvise.

LOGO MISUSE — NEVER DO THIS

The following applications are prohibited without exception. There are no circumstances under which any of the below is acceptable:

	•	Stretch or distort the logo in any axis
	•	Change any colour outside the three approved colourways
	•	Place the logo on a busy photographic background without a protected clear zone
	•	Add drop shadows, glows, gradients, or outlines to any part of the logo
	•	Use the horizontal wordmark without the accompanying crest icon in any digital context
	•	Use the full ornate offline crest in any digital application — website, email, social, or app
	•	Place the logo over a background that does not provide clear legibility
	•	Recreate the logo using different fonts or attempt to redraw the crest from memory
	•	Animate or apply motion to the logo without express sign-off from brand management
	•	Allow any third party, contractor, or supplier to alter the provided logo files
	•	Use a low-resolution version of the logo — always use the provided vector or high-resolution export

Print &
Stationery

Every physical document that leaves the Renaissance Decor studio is a brand experience. The stationery system ensures that a quote, a letterhead, or a compliment slip communicates the same level of care as the finished surface work it accompanies.

BUSINESS CARD

Stock
450–600gsm. Uncoated or soft-touch laminated. Off-white or warm natural stock. Never bright white coated card — it reads as mass-produced.
Size
90×55mm standard. No die cuts, folded cards, or unusual formats. The quality of the material makes the statement.
Front
Split Crest Logo centred on Parchment background. Name in Garamond 10pt below the crest, lightly tracked. Nothing else on the front face.
Back
Charcoal (#3A2A1F) background. White horizontal wordmark top-left. Name, title, contact in Jost 200 Parchment, left-aligned. "Venetian Plaster · Melbourne" in Bark at the foot.
Recommended finish
Spot UV on the crest only (front), or blind deboss of the full ornate crest. Either elevates the card into a premium object. Both together is acceptable and recommended.

LETTERHEAD

Paper
A4. Minimum 120gsm uncoated stock. Parchment, natural, or bright-white — no stark coated paper.
Header
Full ornate crest centred at the top, 32mm wide. "RENAISSANCE DECOR" in Garamond 14pt below, tracked. Double hairline rule in Bark separating header from the body of the letter.
Footer
Address · Phone · Email · Website in Arial 7.5pt Bark, centred. Page number right-aligned.
Body text
Garamond 11pt for formal correspondence. Arial 11pt for technical documents. Charcoal always. 1.6 minimum line height.
Margins
25mm left, 20mm right, 20mm top and bottom — allowing for header and footer.

PRINT SPECIFICATIONS

Colour mode
All print files delivered as CMYK. Never submit RGB files to a commercial print supplier. Spot equivalents: Bark ≈ Pantone 7530 C. Espresso ≈ Pantone 7526 C.
File format
PDF/X-1a for all commercial print. 3mm bleed on all elements that run to the edge. Crop marks and colour bars included.
Image resolution
All images minimum 300dpi at intended print size. Crest and logo files minimum 600dpi or vector EPS.
Proofing
Always request a physical colour proof before any print run above 50 pieces. Digital soft proofs are insufficient for uncoated stock — colour shifts significantly.
Paper approval
For any new stationery application, request a paper dummy before printing. Weight and texture must be approved against the existing business card standard.

Social Media

Social media is where Renaissance Decor demonstrates its work — not where it sells. The feed is a curated portfolio. Every post is a considered editorial decision, not a promotional exercise. The standard for publication is: "Would I be comfortable if Sepand in Toorak or a principal at Sally Caroline Design saw this?" If the answer is not immediately yes, it does not go up.

INSTAGRAM — PRIMARY PLATFORM

Profile image
Option A — Crest Circle. Dark background, full crest, double ring. Upload at 1000×1000px minimum.
Bio copy
"Venetian Plaster  ·  Micro Cement  ·  Clay
Melbourne & Mornington Peninsula
Est. 2004
renaissancedecor.com.au" — No emojis. No hashtags in the bio. Plain text only.
Feed ratio
Square (1:1) or portrait (4:5). Never landscape on the grid. The feed is a gallery — every image is an editorial decision.
Feed aesthetic
Warm, textural, calm. No graphic overlays on project photography. No text-on-image for project posts. Captions carry the story, images carry the emotion.
Caption format
Material. Location. Year. One additional sentence of context if warranted. No hashtag stacks in the caption — maximum 5 relevant tags in the first comment only.
Posting frequency
3–4 posts per week. Quality always over volume. One extraordinary image every two days outperforms daily mediocrity.
Stories
Behind the scenes, process, supplier visits, real-time updates. More conversational in tone — but the voice remains the same. No filters that conflict with the brand palette.
Highlight covers
Use the provided eight Highlight Covers. Each highlight is a curated archive — not a dumping ground for stories. Review and prune quarterly.
Reels
Process videos only. No trending audio. No jump cuts or fast edits. Calm pacing. The material is the subject. Maximum 30 seconds. No voiceover.

CONTENT MATRIX


PUBLISH — YES
	•	Completed project photography
	•	Material and texture detail shots
	•	Architectural context — full room
	•	Collaboration credits (architect, designer)
	•	Process Reels — careful, slow pacing
	•	Industry milestones and anniversaries
	•	Supplier and material origin stories

NEVER PUBLISH
	•	Before/after collage graphics
	•	Any post with pricing information
	•	Emojis in project photo captions
	•	Trending audio on process content
	•	Staff personal content or off-brand moments
	•	Negative competitor references (even implied)
	•	Reposted content without editorial consideration
	•	Promotional graphics with discount or urgency language
